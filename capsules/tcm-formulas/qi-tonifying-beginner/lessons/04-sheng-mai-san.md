# Sheng Mai San (Generate the Pulse Powder)
*Source: Chen & Chen p.593 | Lesson 4 of 4*

---

## Pattern
**Qi and yin deficiency with fluid loss and sweating**

---

## Clinical Manifestations

- Profuse Sweating
- Dry Mouth
- Fatigue
- Palpitations
- Restlessness
- Shortness of Breath
- Thirst
- Weak Pulse

**Tongue:** Red and dry with little coating
**Pulse:** Rapid and weak, or thready and rapid

## Clinical Background

"Sheng Mai" means "generate the pulse" - referring to restoring pulse strength in conditions of qi and yin collapse. ## Distinguishing Features

Sheng Mai San is the **only formula in this group that addresses yin deficiency**. Just three herbs accomplish three things: Ren Shen tonifies qi, Mai Men Dong nourishes yin and moistens lungs, Wu Wei Zi astringes fluids and stops sweating.

Key distinguishing signs:
- **Profuse sweating** + thirst + dry mouth = fluid loss component
- **Red and dry tongue with little coating** (yin deficiency) vs. pale tongue (pure qi deficiency)
- Rapid, thready pulse vs. weak pulse of other qi formulas
- "Generate the pulse" = restoring pulse strength in exhaustion
