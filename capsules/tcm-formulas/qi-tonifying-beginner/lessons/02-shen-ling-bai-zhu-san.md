# Shen Ling Bai Zhu San (Ginseng, Poria, and Atractylodes Powder)
*Source: Chen & Chen p.574 | Lesson 2 of 4*

---

## Pattern
**Spleen qi deficiency with internal dampness accumulation**

---

## Clinical Manifestations

- Chronic diarrhea
- abdominal distension
- edema
- fatigue
- loose stools
- nausea
- poor appetite
- shortness of breath
- weight loss

**Tongue:** Pale with thick white coating
**Pulse:** Deep, weak, and slow

## Actions & Indications

- Tonify spleen qi
- Resolve dampness
- Stop diarrhea
- Strengthen digestion

---

## Clinical Background

"Shen Ling Bai Zhu" represents comprehensive spleen qi tonification with dampness resolution. Classic formula for chronic digestive weakness with internal dampness accumulation. The ten-herb combination addresses both the root (spleen qi deficiency) and the manifestation (dampness and diarrhea). Ess

## Distinguishing Features

Shen Ling Bai Zhu San is **Si Jun Zi Tang plus dampness resolution**. The four-gentlemen base is enhanced with Shan Yao, Lian Zi, Bai Bian Dou, Yi Yi Ren (all spleen-strengthening and dampness-drying), plus Sha Ren to move qi and Jie Geng to raise the qi mechanism.

Key distinguishing signs:
- **Chronic diarrhea** is the hallmark — specifically from spleen qi deficiency + internal dampness
- Pale tongue with **thick white coating** (vs. Si Jun Zi Tang's thin white)
- Ten-herb complexity vs. four-herb simplicity
