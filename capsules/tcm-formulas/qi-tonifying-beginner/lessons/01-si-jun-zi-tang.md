# Si Jun Zi Tang (Four Gentlemen Decoction)
*Source: Chen & Chen p.556 | Lesson 1 of 4*

---

## Pattern
**Spleen qi deficiency**

---

## Clinical Manifestations



**Tongue:** Pale, often with tooth marks, thin white coating
**Pulse:** Weak, especially in the middle position (spleen/stomach)

---

## Composition

| Herb (Pin Yin) | Pharmaceutical Name | Dosage | Role |
|----------------|---------------------|--------|------|
| Ren Shen | Radix et Rhizoma Ginseng | 9-15g | Chief |
| Bai Zhu | Rhizoma Atractylodis Macrocephalae | 9-15g | Deputy |
| Fu Ling | Poria | 9-15g | Assistant |
| Gan Cao | Radix et Rhizoma Glycyrrhizae | 3-6g | Envoy |

---

## Formula Analysis

**Chief (君):** Ren Shen

**Deputy (臣):** Bai Zhu

**Assistant (佐):** Fu Ling

**Envoy (使):** Gan Cao

---

## Actions & Indications

- Tonify qi
- Strengthen spleen
- Harmonize middle jiao

---

## Clinical Background

Foundation formula for all spleen qi deficiency patterns. Represents the "four gentlemen" approach - each herb is mild, harmonious, and supportive rather than aggressive. The perfect balance for building qi without causing stagnation.

---

## Common Modifications

| Condition | Add Herbs | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| With dampness/phlegm | Chen Pi + Ban Xia | Transform phlegm, regulate qi |
| Severe diarrhea | Shan Yao + Lian Zi | Astringe intestines, stop diarrhea |
| Cold constitution | Pao Jiang + Rou Gui | Warm middle jiao |
| Poor appetite | Sha Ren + Chen Pi | Promote appetite, move qi |

---

## Distinguishing Features

Si Jun Zi Tang is the **foundational qi tonic** — the simplest, purest form of spleen qi support. Its power lies in its elegant simplicity: four herbs, each in a defined role, with no contradictions or complex interactions. It is the base from which dozens of more complex formulas are derived. When you see a pure spleen qi deficiency with no phlegm, no dampness, no sinking, no yin deficiency — Si Jun Zi Tang is the answer.

Key distinguishing signs:
- Pure fatigue and poor appetite without significant diarrhea or dampness
- Pale complexion, weak middle pulse
- No organ prolapse, no sweating, no thirst
