# Qi Tonifying Formulas — Flashcards

---

## Si Jun Zi Tang — Pattern
?
**Spleen qi deficiency**: 

---

## Si Jun Zi Tang — Chief Herb
?
Ren Shen (Radix et Rhizoma Ginseng) — Ren Shen

---

## Si Jun Zi Tang — Full Composition
?
Ren Shen [Chief], Bai Zhu [Deputy], Fu Ling [Assistant], Gan Cao [Envoy]

---

## Si Jun Zi Tang — Tongue & Pulse
?
Tongue: Pale, often with tooth marks, thin white coating  
Pulse: Weak, especially in the middle position (spleen/stomach)

---

## Si Jun Zi Tang — Actions
?
Tonify qi; Strengthen spleen; Harmonize middle jiao

---

## Si Jun Zi Tang — English Name
?
Four Gentlemen Decoction

---

## Shen Ling Bai Zhu San — Pattern
?
**Spleen qi deficiency with internal dampness accumulation**: Chronic diarrhea, abdominal distension, edema, fatigue

---

## Shen Ling Bai Zhu San — Chief Herb
?
Ren Shen (Radix et Rhizoma Ginseng), Bai Zhu (Rhizoma Atractylodis Macrocephalae) — Ren Shen + Bai Zhu

---

## Shen Ling Bai Zhu San — Full Composition
?
Ren Shen [Chief], Bai Zhu [Chief], Fu Ling [Deputy], Gan Cao [Deputy], Shan Yao [Assistant], Lian Zi [Assistant], Bai Bian Dou [Assistant], Yi Yi Ren [Assistant], Sha Ren [Envoy], Jie Geng [Envoy]

---

## Shen Ling Bai Zhu San — Tongue & Pulse
?
Tongue: Pale with thick white coating  
Pulse: Deep, weak, and slow

---

## Shen Ling Bai Zhu San — Actions
?
Tonify spleen qi; Resolve dampness; Stop diarrhea; Strengthen digestion

---

## Shen Ling Bai Zhu San — English Name
?
Ginseng, Poria, and Atractylodes Powder

---

## Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang — Pattern
?
**Spleen and stomach qi deficiency with qi sinking**: Chronic Fatigue, Chronic Diarrhea, Dizziness, Poor Appetite

---

## Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang — Chief Herb
?
Huang Qi (Radix Astragali), Ren Shen (Radix et Rhizoma Ginseng) — Huang Qi & Ren Shen

---

## Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang — Full Composition
?
Huang Qi [Chief], Ren Shen [Chief], Bai Zhu [Deputy], Gan Cao [Deputy], Dang Gui [Assistant], Chen Pi [Assistant], Sheng Ma [Envoy], Chai Hu [Envoy]

---

## Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang — Tongue & Pulse
?
Tongue: Pale with thin white coating  
Pulse: Weak, especially in the middle position, may be thready

---

## Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang — Actions
?
Tonify middle jiao qi; Raise yang qi; Lift the clear

---

## Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang — English Name
?
Supplement the Middle and Augment the Qi Decoction

---

## Sheng Mai San — Pattern
?
**Qi and yin deficiency with fluid loss and sweating**: Profuse Sweating, Dry Mouth, Fatigue, Palpitations

---

## Sheng Mai San — Chief Herb
?
Ren Shen (Radix et Rhizoma Ginseng) — Ren Shen

---

## Sheng Mai San — Full Composition
?
Ren Shen [Chief], Mai Men Dong [Deputy], Wu Wei Zi [Assistant]

---

## Sheng Mai San — Tongue & Pulse
?
Tongue: Red and dry with little coating  
Pulse: Rapid and weak, or thready and rapid

---

## Sheng Mai San — Actions
?
Tonify qi; Nourish yin; Generate fluids; Astringe sweat

---

## Sheng Mai San — English Name
?
Generate the Pulse Powder

---

## Qi Tonifying Formulas — Prolapse Indicator
?
Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang — the only qi tonic in this group with lifting herbs (Sheng Ma + Chai Hu), specifically indicated for organ prolapse.

---

## Qi Tonifying Formulas — Chronic Diarrhea + Dampness
?
Shen Ling Bai Zhu San — Si Jun Zi Tang base plus dampness-resolving herbs (Yi Yi Ren, Bai Bian Dou, Lian Zi, Shan Yao) and Jie Geng to raise qi.

---

## Qi Tonifying Formulas — Only 3 Herbs
?
Sheng Mai San — Ren Shen + Mai Men Dong + Wu Wei Zi. "Generate the pulse": qi + yin + astringe.

---

## Qi Tonifying Formulas — Red Dry Tongue
?
Sheng Mai San — the only formula in this group that addresses yin deficiency. Red dry tongue with little coating indicates fluid loss.
