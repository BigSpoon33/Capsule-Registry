# Quiz: Qi Tonifying Formulas

## Q1: What is the primary pattern treated by Si Jun Zi Tang?
- [ ] A. Kidney Qi Deficiency
- [x] B. Spleen Qi Deficiency
- [ ] C. Lung Qi Deficiency with Sweating
- [ ] D. Spleen Qi Deficiency with Organ Prolapse

*Explanation: Si Jun Zi Tang tonifies Spleen Qi and strengthens the middle jiao. It is the foundational formula for pure spleen qi deficiency without dampness, sinking, or yin deficiency.*

## Q2: Which herb serves as the Chief in Si Jun Zi Tang?
- [ ] A. Bai Zhu
- [ ] B. Fu Ling
- [x] C. Ren Shen
- [ ] D. Gan Cao

*Explanation: Ren Shen (Radix et Rhizoma Ginseng) is the Chief in Si Jun Zi Tang — it powerfully tonifies yuan qi and strengthens the spleen. Bai Zhu is Deputy, Fu Ling is Assistant, and Gan Cao is Envoy.*

## Q3: Si Jun Zi Tang is the base formula for which of the following?
- [ ] A. Sheng Mai San
- [ ] B. Xiao Chai Hu Tang
- [x] C. Liu Jun Zi Tang
- [ ] D. Zhi Bai Di Huang Wan

*Explanation: Si Jun Zi Tang is the base for Liu Jun Zi Tang (+ Chen Pi + Ban Xia for phlegm-dampness), Xiang Sha Liu Jun Zi Tang, and Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang. It is the "mother of all qi tonics."*

## Q4: Which tongue presentation is classic for Si Jun Zi Tang patterns?
- [ ] A. Red and dry with little coating
- [ ] B. Purple with stasis spots
- [x] C. Pale with tooth marks and thin white coating
- [ ] D. Red with yellow greasy coating

*Explanation: Pale tongue with tooth marks indicates spleen qi deficiency — the spleen is too weak to hold fluids in proper form, leaving tooth impressions on the tongue edges. Thin white coating indicates no heat.*

## Q5: Shen Ling Bai Zhu San is best described as:
- [ ] A. Si Jun Zi Tang plus lifting herbs for prolapse
- [x] B. Si Jun Zi Tang plus dampness-resolving herbs for chronic diarrhea
- [ ] C. Three herbs for qi-yin deficiency with sweating
- [ ] D. A formula for spleen qi sinking with organ prolapse

*Explanation: Shen Ling Bai Zhu San = Si Jun Zi Tang base (Ren Shen, Bai Zhu, Fu Ling, Gan Cao) plus Shan Yao, Lian Zi, Bai Bian Dou, Yi Yi Ren for dampness resolution, Sha Ren for qi movement, and Jie Geng to raise qi mechanism.*

## Q6: Which herb in Shen Ling Bai Zhu San is responsible for raising the qi mechanism?
- [ ] A. Sha Ren
- [x] B. Jie Geng
- [ ] C. Yi Yi Ren
- [ ] D. Lian Zi

*Explanation: Jie Geng (Radix Platycodonis) raises the qi mechanism, directing the other herbs upward and aiding in the formula's distribution. Sha Ren moves qi and aromatically awakens the spleen.*

## Q7: Which tongue/pulse presentation distinguishes Shen Ling Bai Zhu San's pattern from Si Jun Zi Tang's?
- [ ] A. Red dry tongue vs. pale tongue
- [x] B. Thick white coating vs. thin white coating
- [ ] C. Yellow greasy coating vs. thin white coating
- [ ] D. Pale purple tongue vs. pale tongue

*Explanation: The thick white coating in Shen Ling Bai Zhu San patterns reflects internal dampness accumulation, distinguishing it from the thin white coating of simple Si Jun Zi Tang patterns.*

## Q8: A patient presents with chronic diarrhea, weight loss, fatigue, abdominal distension, and edema. Tongue: pale with thick white coating. Pulse: deep, weak. Which formula is most appropriate?
- [ ] A. Si Jun Zi Tang
- [ ] B. Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
- [x] C. Shen Ling Bai Zhu San
- [ ] D. Sheng Mai San

*Explanation: Chronic diarrhea + dampness signs (edema, thick white coating) = Spleen Qi Deficiency with internal dampness → Shen Ling Bai Zhu San. Si Jun Zi Tang lacks dampness-resolving herbs; Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang is for prolapse; Sheng Mai San is for qi-yin deficiency with sweating.*

## Q9: Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang's key distinguishing indication not shared by Si Jun Zi Tang is:
- [ ] A. Chronic fatigue
- [ ] B. Poor appetite
- [x] C. Organ prolapse (uterus, rectum, stomach)
- [ ] D. Loose stools

*Explanation: Organ prolapse (uterine, rectal, gastric) is the hallmark of Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang. The lifting herbs Sheng Ma and Chai Hu raise sinking yang qi, counteracting the downward movement of prolapse. Si Jun Zi Tang tonifies qi but cannot lift what has sunk.*

## Q10: Which pair of herbs in Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang performs the lifting action?
- [ ] A. Huang Qi + Ren Shen
- [ ] B. Dang Gui + Chen Pi
- [x] C. Sheng Ma + Chai Hu
- [ ] D. Bai Zhu + Gan Cao

*Explanation: Sheng Ma (Rhizoma Cimicifugae) and Chai Hu (Radix Bupleuri) are the Envoy herbs that raise yang qi and lift the clear, directly addressing the sinking qi mechanism. They are present in very small doses (3-6g) relative to the chief herbs.*

## Q11: Which herb serves as the primary Chief in Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang (at the highest dose)?
- [ ] A. Ren Shen
- [ ] B. Bai Zhu
- [x] C. Huang Qi
- [ ] D. Dang Gui

*Explanation: Huang Qi (Radix Astragali) is the primary Chief at 15-30g — the highest dose in the formula. It powerfully tonifies spleen and lung qi and stabilizes the exterior. Ren Shen is co-Chief, but Huang Qi leads the formula.*

## Q12: A patient has uterine prolapse, chronic fatigue that worsens after meals, shortness of breath, and a weak, thready pulse in the middle position. Which formula is indicated?
- [ ] A. Shen Ling Bai Zhu San
- [x] B. Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
- [ ] C. Si Jun Zi Tang
- [ ] D. Sheng Mai San

*Explanation: Uterine prolapse + fatigue worsening after meals + weak middle pulse = Spleen/Stomach Qi Deficiency with Qi Sinking → Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang. The worsening after meals is a key differentiator (the digestive effort further depletes already-sinking qi).*

## Q13: What does "Sheng Mai" literally mean in English?
- [ ] A. Supplement the Middle
- [x] B. Generate the Pulse
- [ ] C. Tonify Qi and Yin
- [ ] D. Stop Sweating and Generate Fluids

*Explanation: "Sheng" = generate/restore, "Mai" = pulse/vessel. The name refers to restoring pulse strength in conditions of qi and yin collapse — the pulse becomes weak and thready when both qi and yin are severely depleted.*

## Q14: Sheng Mai San is uniquely distinguished from the other three qi tonifying formulas by its treatment of:
- [ ] A. Spleen qi deficiency with dampness
- [ ] B. Organ prolapse from qi sinking
- [x] C. Qi and yin deficiency with fluid loss
- [ ] D. Pure spleen qi deficiency

*Explanation: Sheng Mai San is the only formula in this group that addresses yin deficiency. Mai Men Dong nourishes yin and generates fluids, while Wu Wei Zi astringes fluids to stop sweating. The other three formulas all treat pure qi deficiency patterns without yin involvement.*

## Q15: Which tongue presentation indicates Sheng Mai San over the other qi tonifying formulas?
- [ ] A. Pale with thin white coating
- [ ] B. Pale with thick white coating
- [x] C. Red and dry with little coating
- [ ] D. Pale with teeth marks

*Explanation: Red and dry tongue with little coating = yin deficiency with fluid loss — the hallmark of Sheng Mai San patterns. All other qi tonifying formulas in this group present with pale tongues reflecting pure qi deficiency.*

## Q16: A patient presents with profuse sweating, shortness of breath, fatigue, dry mouth, thirst, and palpitations in the summer heat. Tongue: red, dry. Pulse: rapid, thready. Which formula is indicated?
- [ ] A. Si Jun Zi Tang
- [ ] B. Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
- [ ] C. Shen Ling Bai Zhu San
- [x] D. Sheng Mai San

*Explanation: Profuse sweating + thirst + dry mouth + palpitations + red dry tongue + rapid thready pulse = Qi and Yin Deficiency with fluid loss → Sheng Mai San. This presentation is classic for summer heat exhaustion or any condition where both qi and fluids are simultaneously depleted.*
