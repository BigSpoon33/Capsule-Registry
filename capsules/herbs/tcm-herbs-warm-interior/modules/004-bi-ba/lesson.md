## Overview
**Bi Ba**, pharmaceutical name *Piperis Longi Fructus* (long pepper fruit), belongs to the **Piperaceae** family and is classified as an herb that **warms the interior**. As noted in *Chinese Herbal Medicine: Materia Medica*, this herb entered the materia medica during the Kaibao Era. Although historical texts sometimes confused it with *Litsea cubeba* (cheng qie zi) due to their shared pepper-like qualities, the current standard refers specifically to the fruit of *Piper longum* L. The herb is characterized by its **acrid** taste and **hot** temperature. It enters the **Stomach** and **Large Intestine** channels, making it a potent agent for addressing cold pathogenic factors trapped within the internal organs.

## Key Concepts
The primary action of Bi Ba is to **warm the middle burner** and **release constrained cold** specifically from the Stomach and Intestines. Its hot nature allows it to disperse congealed cold that leads to **qi stagnation** in the epigastrium and abdomen, thereby alleviating pain. 

When studying this herb, students must note its traditional contraindications clearly defined in *Encountering the Sources of the Classic of Materia Medica*. It is strictly **forbidden** for patients presenting with:
*   **Yin deficiency**
*   **Heat in the blood level**
*   **Fever and cough**

Because of its hot nature, Bi Ba is not suitable for those who already possess internal heat or dryness. The dosage for this herb typically ranges from **1.5 to 4.5 grams**. Regarding preparation, while ancient texts described complex steaming and soaking processes, modern usage generally involves using the fruit without removing the stems or pericarp, though it may be pulverized.

## Clinical Relevance
In clinical practice, Bi Ba is indicated for conditions where cold has congealed in the digestive tract, causing obstruction and pain. The herb is particularly effective for **cold congealing** that manifests as pain in the upper and middle abdomen. 

Clinicians should consider Bi Ba when a patient presents with symptoms of cold stagnation in the Stomach and Large Intestine. Key clinical features to look for include:
*   **Painful obstruction** in the epigastrium or abdomen that worsens with cold.
*   **Distended and painful lower abdomen** associated with cold patterns.
*   General **stomach aches** driven by cold invasion.

The herb's ability to warm the interior makes it a targeted choice for cold-damp or pure cold patterns affecting the digestive system, provided the patient does not have the contraindicated heat syndromes. Its acrid nature helps to move qi and break up the stagnation caused by the cold, restoring normal flow in the affected channels.

## Summary
**Bi Ba** (*Piperis Longi Fructus*) is an **acrid, hot** herb that enters the **Stomach** and **Large Intestine** channels. Its core function is to **warm the middle**, **disperse cold**, and **alleviate pain** caused by cold congealing and subsequent **qi stagnation**. While historically linked to other pepper-like fruits, it is now standardized as *Piper longum*. It is contraindicated in cases of **yin deficiency**, **heat in the blood**, or **fever with cough**. Clinicians should prescribe it for abdominal pain and digestive cold patterns, adhering to a dosage of 1.5–4.5g, while strictly avoiding its use in heat-deficiency contexts.
