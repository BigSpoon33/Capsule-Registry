## What are the taste and temperature characteristics of Bi Ba?
?
Bi Ba has an acrid taste and a hot temperature, which make it a potent agent for warming the interior and dispersing cold pathogenic factors.

---

## Which two body channels does Bi Ba enter?
?
Bi Ba enters the Stomach and Large Intestine channels, allowing it to specifically target cold pathogenic factors trapped within the digestive organs.

---

## What is the primary therapeutic action of Bi Ba regarding cold and qi stagnation?
?
Bi Ba warms the middle burner and releases constrained cold from the stomach and intestines, thereby alleviating qi stagnation and pain in the epigastrium and abdomen.

---

## What are the three main contraindications for using Bi Ba?
?
Bi Ba is strictly forbidden for patients with Yin deficiency, heat in the blood level, or symptoms of fever and cough due to its hot nature.

---

## What are the typical dosage ranges for Bi Ba in grams?
?
The standard dosage for Bi Ba ranges from 1.5 to 4.5 grams, though modern usage typically involves using the fruit with the stems or pericarp intact.

---

## Which specific abdominal symptom pattern indicates the clinical use of Bi Ba?
?
Bi Ba is indicated for painful obstruction in the epigastrium or abdomen that worsens with cold, as well as a distended and painful lower abdomen associated with cold patterns.

---

## How does Bi Ba address pain caused by cold congealing?
?
By warming the interior and dispersing congealed cold, Bi Ba resolves the qi stagnation that leads to pain in the upper and middle abdomen.

---

## What botanical family does Bi Ba belong to, and what is its pharmaceutical name?
?
Bi Ba belongs to the Piperaceae family and its pharmaceutical name is Piperis Longi Fructus, referring to the fruit of Piper longum L.

---

## Why is Bi Ba unsuitable for patients who already possess internal heat or dryness?
?
Because Bi Ba is acrid and hot, it would exacerbate existing internal heat or dryness, making it inappropriate for these specific pathological conditions.
