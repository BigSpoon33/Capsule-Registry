## 1. Differentiating Heart Yang Deficiency Patterns

**Question:**
A patient presents with chest tightness, pale complexion, and a slow, deep pulse. You must decide whether to use the general *Rou Gui* (Cinnamon Bark) or the specific *Rou Gui Xin* (Cinnamon Heart). How do you distinguish the appropriate form based on the clinical presentation and the herb's channel specificities?

**Key Concepts to Cover:**
- *Rou Gui Xin* is the preferred form for specifically targeting Heart disorders, whereas general *Rou Gui* addresses broader systemic deficiencies.
- The herb warms Heart Yang to disperse chest painful obstructions caused by cold and promotes blood movement within the vessels.
- Clinical differentiation requires recognizing when cold obstruction is localized to the Heart versus a general systemic Yang deficiency.

**Assessment Criteria:**
- The response must identify *Rou Gui Xin* as the specific choice for Heart channel pathology (chest pain, Heart Yang deficiency).
- The explanation should articulate that while general *Rou Gui* treats systemic cold, the heart-specific form is indicated when the primary presentation involves Heart Yang insufficiency and chest obstruction.

**Reference Content:**
In cases specifically targeting Heart disorders, the preferred form is Rou Gui Xin (Cinnamon heart), though the general cortex addresses broader systemic deficiencies. Rou Gui warms Heart Yang and disperses chest painful obstruction caused by cold, promoting blood movement within the vessels. Clinically, Rou Gui is rarely used alone for Heart Yang insufficiency; it is typically combined with appropriate assistant or envoy herbs to target the specific organ.

## 2. Managing False Heat via Yin Congealment

**Question:**
A patient presents with an aversion to cold, cold limbs, yet exhibits floating, superficial heat signs and nocturia. How do you apply Rou Gui to resolve this apparent contradiction, and what is the specific mechanism regarding Yin and Yang interaction you are utilizing?

**Key Concepts to Cover:**
- Rou Gui guides weak Yang back to its root when Yin influence in the lower burner forces Yang outward, creating manifestations of false heat.
- The acrid warmth of Rou Gui disperses the congealed Yin influence, allowing the weak Yang to return to its proper position.
- This application is distinct from treating false heat caused solely by Yang weakness without the congealed Yin factor.

**Assessment Criteria:**
- The answer must demonstrate the understanding that the "false heat" is actually weak Yang forced outward by excessive Yin in the lower burner.
- The response should correctly identify that Rou Gui works by dispersing the congealed Yin to allow Yang to return, rather than simply tonifying Yang in isolation.

**Reference Content:**
A unique application involves guiding weak Yang back to its root. When Yin influence is excessive in the lower burner, it can force weak Yang outward, creating manifestations of false heat. In this scenario, the acrid warmth of Rou Gui can disperse the congealed Yin influence, allowing Yang to return. However, practitioners must note that some views suggest it is not ideal if the floating Yang is due solely to Yang weakness without the congealed Yin factor.
