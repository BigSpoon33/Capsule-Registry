## Overview
**Rou Gui** (*Cinnamomi Cortex*) is a potent herb classified under those that Warm the Interior and Expel Cold. Its nature is sweet, acrid, and very warm, creating a dynamic profile where its sweetness tonifies while its acridity disperses. This powerful warming quality allows it to strongly unblock cold obstructions. The herb enters the Heart, Spleen, Liver, and Kidney channels, making it a versatile agent for conditions involving cold stagnation across these systems. In cases specifically targeting Heart disorders, the preferred form is **Rou Gui Xin** (Cinnamon heart), though the general cortex addresses broader systemic deficiencies.

## Key Concepts
The therapeutic mechanism of Rou Gui revolves around its ability to warm Yang and move blood. Because the pulse is often slow in cold conditions, the use of this herb should always be considered when encountering such a sign. Its actions include:

*   **Heart:** Warms Heart Yang and disperses chest painful obstruction caused by cold, promoting blood movement within the vessels.
*   **Spleen and Kidney:** Warms the Spleen to treat loss of appetite and loose stools with undigested food due to Spleen Yang deficiency. It is even more appropriate for Kidney Yang deficiency.
*   **Liver and Blood:** Enters the blood aspect of the Liver to disperse blood stasis. It unblocks menstruation in cases of amenorrhea, alleviates menstrual pain due to cold, and breaks up both mobile and fixed abdominal masses.
*   **Kidney and Gate of Vitality:** Tonifies Kidney Yang and the fire at the gate of vitality. This treats symptoms such as cold weakness of the lower back, chilled extremities, and nocturia.
*   **Channel Pathology:** Treats painful obstructions due to cold and dampness, including joint pain throughout the body.

A unique application involves guiding weak Yang back to its root. When Yin influence is excessive in the lower burner, it can force weak Yang outward, creating manifestations of false heat. In this scenario, the acrid warmth of Rou Gui can disperse the congealed Yin influence, allowing Yang to return. However, practitioners must note that some views suggest it is not ideal if the floating Yang is due solely to Yang weakness without the congealed Yin factor.

## Clinical Relevance
Clinically, Rou Gui is essential for dispelling deep-seated cold. Historical texts describe a method where Rou Gui, combined with **Hua Jiao** (*Zanthoxyli Pericarpium*) and **Gan Jiang** (*Zingiberis Rhizoma*), are soaked in wine. Bandages soaked in this mixture are wrapped around painful areas with heat applied, leveraging the herb's ability to unblock channels and disperse wind-cold and dampness.

When treating Heart Yang insufficiency or augmenting Spleen Yang, Rou Gui is rarely used alone; it is typically combined with appropriate assistant or envoy herbs to target the specific organ. Its aromatic, piercing warmth makes it effective for breaking up blood clumping and expelling masses that have become fixed due to cold stasis.

## Summary
Rou Gui is a critical herb for warming the interior and expelling cold through the Heart, Spleen, Liver, and Kidney channels. Its dual nature of tonifying sweetness and dispersing acridity allows it to treat a wide spectrum of cold-induced pathologies, from digestive stagnation and menstrual pain to lower back weakness and false heat caused by Yin excess. By guiding fire back to its origin and unblocking blood stasis, it remains a cornerstone for managing conditions characterized by slow pulses and cold obstruction.
